CST 373

Ethics in Communication & Technology 
Investigates through an ethical perspective how communication technology affects our lives. Discusses individual and institutional values represented through technological choices. Using case studies and current events, explores such issues as intellectual property rights, information access and privacy, and the digital divide.

Ethics in Communication & Technology is a course that teaches students how to have proper discipline in ethical matters. Every week of the course students were required to create a scrapbook entry about a recent news article with an ethical issue hanging in the balance. Students were to write a summary of an article they read and then respond to the article on how it holds and ethical issue at stake and the students opinion on the matter. The course consisted of a number of group-written papers that surround an ethical issue. The final paper is an website with sections built into links formed by the class as a whole. The e-paper is meant for students to look deep into their chosen topic, the history, the different views on the subject matter and why you believe it to be an ethical issue worth addressing.
