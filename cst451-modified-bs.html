CST 451

Web Production
Web-based design and project management continuing the development of skills and techniques introduced in CST 351. Includes integration of current web technologies and techniques to produce a fully functional website for a client. Topics include Content Management Systems, Project Management, and Usability and Testing.

Web Production is the final course in the trilogy of emphasis classes that introduces CMS and the steps involved from start to finish of website production. Using the entire classas a production team, our teacher Kevin Cahill leads the class project of designing an actual site for a real client, based off of their needs and agenda using Drupal via Pantheon.io. Over the course of the semester our class meets with the clients weekly to learn about who and how they want to their site to serve.

Due to certain classes as well as the prerequisites for those classes being offered only once a year, My academic advisor had me take Web Production twice; the first before taking CST 351, Web Design where I worked under guidance, becoming familiar with Drupal CMS, and using Adobe Illustrator to design the sites concept artwork. The second time in the course I was a group leader, helping to guide and teach my underclassmen how to use Drupal and make back or front-end changes.
